🏢 **Corporate first.** FTSE 250, publicly traded — admin at the highest levels: finance, HR at Hays, facilities and office management. That's where I learned how systems actually work.

🧘 **Then I walked away.** Corporate greed had hollowed too much out, so I built a wellness business solo — studios, videos, a one-man show. It worked.

🌊 **Three years ago I went all-in on AI.** Tool-agnostic, bought and tried everything. What I found: AI is quietly democratising the enterprise-grade power small owners were always priced out of — but it isn't reaching them. They use frontier models generically and get back the average of the internet. **They have the access. They don't have the leverage.**

> [!TIP]
> The tools are no longer the constraint — they're already better than most people use them. The constraint is **personalization.** That's where I work.

I think the SaaS era is closing. What's next is **personal operating systems** — your tools, configured around how you actually work, not a generic install.

### 🛠️ That's the gap I close

- **For the many** — small open-source tools, built in public.
- **For business owners** — I configure their AI properly (architecture, skills, scaffolding) so they claw back hours and money, **in their own voice.**

🤝 **My tribe:** solo founders building a decent living serving real people — especially in wellness, because I was one.

📍 Barcelona · Claude is my current tool of choice (its configuration model is the best I've used for this), but the fluency is cross-vendor.
